Course Spotlight: LawLI-05
Constitution & Law for Development Practice
"Constitutional
literacy is
development
literacy."
13 modules grounded in real case law. Granville Austin · Upendra Baxi · Rohini Pande.
~6 Hours · Free · impactmojo.in/courses/law
Caption
Every development practitioner working on NREGA, the Food Security Act, the Forest Rights Act, or RTI is working with constitutional provisions every day. Most have never actually read those provisions. That's not surprising. Legal literacy has never been included in development sector training. But it should be. Understanding why the basic structure doctrine prevents certain amendments, how PIL works in practice, why Article 21 keeps expanding, and how environmental law intersects with resource rights — this changes how you design programs. Not because you're doing legal work, but because the rights infrastructure shapes what's feasible. Constitution and Law for Development Practice: 13 modules grounded in real case law. Covers the basic structure doctrine, PIL, Article 21, reservations, forest rights, digital rights, and climate justice. Built on the scholarship of Granville Austin, Upendra Baxi, and Rohini Pande. Free. ~6 hours. → impactmojo.in/courses/law #ConstitutionalLaw #DevelopmentPractice #RightsBasedApproach #India #PolicyResearch

ImpactMojo's voice — 5 principles
Specific over vague
Name the number. Name the course. Name the case study. "500+ development sector terms, no login required" beats "we have lots of resources" every time.
Practitioner-first
Always start from what the practitioner is experiencing. The training gap, the jargon problem, the indicator confusion. ImpactMojo is the answer; the problem comes first.
South Asia context
Reference MGNREGA, ASER, RTI, PARI, NITI Aayog. This signals to the audience that the platform was built for them, not adapted from something else.
No filler confidence
Don't soften claims with "we believe" or "we hope." State what the platform does and let that stand. "48 free courses" needs no qualifier.
Free is the differentiator
Lead with free more than you're comfortable with. In a sector where training costs tens of thousands of rupees, free is not just a price point — it's a position.
Vandana's personal touch
Add one line from her own experience at the start or end. This makes institutional content feel human rather than broadcast.
Phrases to avoid in all captions
✗ "Excited to share..."✗ "We believe learning should be accessible to all"✗ "Thrilled / delighted / honoured"✗ "Game-changing / transformative"✗ "In today's fast-paced world..."✗ "Don't miss out!"✗ "Empowering practitioners"✗ "Check out our amazing platform!"✗ Long unrelated hashtag strings
Sample personal-voice opener for Vandana (LinkedIn)
"In 15 years of working on capacity building in India's development sector, I've watched the same gap repeat itself. Program managers who are doing sophisticated work — designing surveys, managing evaluations, engaging with government policy — who have never had access to the training that would let them do it more rigorously.

Not because they didn't want to learn. Because the training that exists is either behind expensive institutions or designed for someone else's context entirely.

That's what we built ImpactMojo to fix."
Works as an opener before LI-01, LI-03, or LI-10.

Getting images out of this kit
  • Click any visual to expand it full-size in the modal overlay.
  • Screenshot the expanded image using your OS screenshot tool (Cmd+Shift+4 on Mac, Snipping Tool on Windows).
  • For carousels, screenshot each slide individually using the arrow navigation, then upload all as a multi-image post on Instagram.
  • LinkedIn accepts images at the LinkedIn post aspect ratio — the expanded LinkedIn visuals are the right proportions.

Brochure distribution
  • Email to NGO L&D leads, HR contacts, university coordinators. Attach the PDF directly — do not send a download link.
  • Subject: "Free development training for [org] teams — ImpactMojo"
  • Follow up after 5–7 days if no response. One follow-up is standard in the sector.
  • Upload to impactmojo.in as a downloadable on the About or Partners page.
  • Pin to the WhatsApp PLC.
📊
Measuring what's working
  • LinkedIn: track impressions and profile clicks per post. Course posts should drive clicks to the site. Institutional posts should generate DMs or connection requests.
  • Instagram: carousel saves and shares are the most meaningful metric. A saved carousel means someone intends to use it.
  • After 4 weeks, identify the 3 posts with the most engagement and create follow-up content in the same format.
🔁
Reusing this content
  • All 25 assets can be reposted after 8–12 weeks. Social media audiences turn over quickly.
  • Update stat figures (courses, learners, etc.) before each repost cycle.
  • Mix in personal posts from Vandana between kit assets — roughly 1 personal post per 2–3 kit posts keeps the account human.
  • New course launches, game releases, and resource additions are natural triggers for fresh posts following the same visual style.
🤝
Partnership outreach sequence
  • Step 1: LinkedIn connection request + message referencing something specific about their work
  • Step 2: 3–5 days later, email with brochure PDF attached and 2-line personalised context
  • Step 3: If no response in 7 days, one follow-up: "Sending this once more in case it got buried"
  • Step 4: Move on. One more attempt in 3 months when there's a new hook.
