# Vertical Shorts / Reels — The Heat Floor

3 cut-down concepts. Each: 9:16 vertical, 30–60 seconds, hook in first 2 seconds, single payoff, single CTA.

---

## Short #1 — "The 17× Problem" (45s)

**Hook (0–2s):** Big text appears: **"Same temperature. 17× the loss."**

**Body (2–35s):**
> "Last year, *Nature* published the cleanest macro estimate we have for what heat does to Indian labor. The number that should change how we think about industrial policy: informal-sector workers in South India are seventeen times more likely to lose earnings on heat-stressed days than formal-sector workers exposed to the same temperature. Same temperature. 17×.
>
> Now: industrial policy in South Asia runs at twice the rate of other emerging markets. Half goes to manufacturing. The workers it's supposed to absorb? They're the 17×."

**Visual:** Stacked bar (informal vs formal), then zoom out to factory map.

**CTA (35–45s):** *"Full breakdown — link in bio. ImpactMojo."*

**Use:** Threads, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts.

## Short #2 — "What the WB Report Skipped" (60s)

**Hook (0–2s):** *"The World Bank told South Asia to do more industrial policy. Here's what they didn't measure."*

**Body (2–50s):**
> "April 8, 2026. The World Bank's South Asia Economic Update lands. Headline: growth slows to 6.3 percent. Underneath: South Asia uses industrial policy at twice the rate of peer economies. The Bank's verdict — keep going, just get better at it.
>
> Here's the missing variable. India loses 259 billion working hours per year to heat. A one-degree rise in wet-bulb temperature cuts earnings by 19 percent. Adaptation — fans, partial cooling — only partially closes the gap. The JPE proved this in 2021.
>
> So when industrial policy targets manufacturing, it's targeting the sector with a heat floor it doesn't budget for."

**Visual:** WB report cover → Nature paper → JPE paper → factory floor with thermometer.

**CTA (50–60s):** *"Three redesigns in the full video. ImpactMojo. Link in bio."*

**Use:** LinkedIn (high-context audience), YouTube Shorts.

## Production notes

- All three: same typeface, same color palette as the long-form video.
- Captions burned in (not auto-generated). Vertical safe-zone: keep text within centre 60% of frame.
- One re-hook per short, around the 2/3 mark — text appears: "Wait — there's one more thing."
- Background music: low bed, no lyrics, fade out under final CTA.
- Each short ends with the same end card: "Full breakdown → link in bio · ImpactMojo".
