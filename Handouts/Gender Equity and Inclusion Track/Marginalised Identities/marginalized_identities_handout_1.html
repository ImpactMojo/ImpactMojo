Marginalized Identities 101

Understanding Intersectionality, Rights, and Representation in South Asian Development
ImpactMojo Workshop Series • Handout 1: Foundations of Identity and Intersectionality
75-90 Minutes

Workshop Overview

Target Audience: Development practitioners, social researchers, policy analysts, community organizers, and advocacy professionals

Prerequisites: Basic understanding of development concepts and Indian constitutional framework

Materials Needed: Notebooks, sticky notes, flip chart paper, markers, copies of constitutional articles

Learning Objectives

By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:

  • Apply intersectional analysis to understand multiple, overlapping forms of marginalization
  • Analyze how caste, tribe, gender, religion, disability, and other identities shape lived experiences
  • Use constitutional and legal frameworks to support identity-based rights claims
  • Design inclusive development interventions that address intersectional discrimination
  • Evaluate representation and inclusion in policies, programs, and institutions

Part 1: The Reality of Multiple Identities

20 minutes

🏠 Meera's Story: When Identities Intersect

Background: Meera is a 28-year-old Dalit woman from rural Karnataka who uses a wheelchair due to polio. She works as a data entry operator for the local panchayat.

Daily Realities:

  • At the Workplace: Colleagues avoid sharing meals, and the office lacks wheelchair accessibility
  • In Healthcare: Doctors often speak to her male relatives rather than directly to her
  • During Elections: Polling booth is on the second floor with no elevator
  • In Marriage: Families reject proposals citing both her caste and disability
  • In Banking: Struggles to get loans despite steady income and good credit

Question for Discussion: Which of Meera's identities is causing discrimination in each situation? Can we separate them?

Understanding Intersectionality

🔍 Kimberlé Crenshaw's Intersectionality Framework

Key Insight: "The intersection of racism and sexism factors into Black women's lives in ways that cannot be captured wholly by looking at the race or gender dimensions of those experiences separately."

Applied to Indian Context:

  • Multiplicative Effects: Being Dalit + Woman + Disabled = unique discrimination, not just sum of parts
  • Invisible Experiences: Mainstream feminism misses caste, mainstream Dalit movements miss gender
  • Policy Gaps: Anti-discrimination laws address single identities, miss intersectional experiences

🎯 Mapping Identity Intersections (8 minutes)

Individual Reflection (3 minutes):

  1. Write down 5 aspects of your social identity (caste, religion, gender, class, region, language, ability, etc.)
  2. Mark which identities give you privilege (+) and which create disadvantage (-)
  3. Identify one situation where multiple identities shaped your experience

Pair Discussion (5 minutes):

  • Share one example of how identities combined to create advantage OR disadvantage
  • Discuss: How do development programs address (or miss) these intersections?

Part 2: Major Identity Categories in Indian Context

25 minutes
🏛️ Caste Identity

Constitutional Status: SCs (16.6%), STs (8.6%), OBCs (~40%)

Key Issues: Untouchability, educational access, political representation, inter-caste marriage

Legal Framework: Articles 15, 16, 17; Atrocities Act 1989

🏞️ Tribal Identity

Population: 705 tribes across India, 10.43 crore people

Key Issues: Land rights, displacement, cultural preservation, forest access

Legal Framework: Fifth Schedule, PESA 1996, FRA 2006

⚧️ Gender & Sexuality

Categories: Women, transgender persons, sexual minorities

Key Issues: Violence, economic participation, political representation, healthcare access

Legal Framework: NALSA judgment 2014, Transgender Persons Act 2019

🕊️ Religious Identity

Major Groups: Muslims (14.2%), Christians (2.3%), Sikhs (1.7%), others

Key Issues: Communal violence, educational access, employment discrimination

Legal Framework: Articles 25-28, minority institutions protection

♿ Disability

Population: 2.68 crore persons (2011 Census)

Key Issues: Accessibility, employment, education, healthcare, assistive technology

Legal Framework: Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act 2016

🗣️ Linguistic Identity

Diversity: 22 official languages, 1,600+ dialects

Key Issues: Medium of instruction, government services, cultural preservation

Legal Framework: Article 350, Official Languages Act

Intersectional Discrimination Patterns

📊 Key Statistics on Intersectional Disadvantage

Identity Intersection Specific Disadvantage Data Point
Dalit Women Sexual violence rates 3x higher than upper-caste women (NCRB data)
Muslim Women Workforce participation 8.8% vs 25.8% Hindu women (NSSO)
Tribal Girls School dropout rates Higher at every education level (ASER)
Disabled Women Marriage rates Lower marriage, higher abandonment rates
Transgender SC/ST Reservation access Limited access to caste-based reservations

🎯 Analyzing Intersectional Barriers (12 minutes)

Case Study: Rural Self-Help Group Formation

Scenario: A development NGO wants to form women's self-help groups in a multi-caste village in Uttar Pradesh.

Small Group Analysis (groups of 4-5, 8 minutes):

  1. Identify barriers (3 min): What challenges might different groups of women face?
    • Upper-caste women
    • OBC women
    • Dalit women
    • Muslim women
    • Women with disabilities
  2. Design solutions (3 min): How would you ensure inclusive participation?
  3. Anticipate resistance (2 min): What pushback might you face and from whom?

Report Back (4 minutes): Each group shares one insight about intersectional barriers

Part 3: Constitutional and Legal Frameworks

15 minutes

⚖️ Constitutional Foundation for Identity Rights

Fundamental Rights (Part III):

  • Article 14: Equality before law and equal protection of laws
  • Article 15: Prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth
  • Article 16: Equality of opportunity in public employment
  • Article 17: Abolition of untouchability
  • Article 19: Freedom of speech, assembly, movement, and residence
  • Articles 25-28: Freedom of religion and conscience

Directive Principles (Part IV):

  • Article 46: Promotion of educational and economic interests of SCs, STs
  • Article 47: Duty to raise level of nutrition and standard of living

Special Provisions:

  • Article 330: Reservation of seats for SCs and STs in Lok Sabha
  • Article 335: Claims of SCs and STs in services and posts
  • Fifth and Sixth Schedules: Administration of tribal areas

Major Legislative Protections

📜 Key Laws for Identity-Based Rights

Anti-Discrimination Laws:

  • SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act 1989: Criminal penalties for caste-based violence
  • Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act 2013: Protection in employment
  • Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act 2016: Comprehensive disability rights

Representation and Participation:

  • Panchayats (Extension to Scheduled Areas) Act 1996: Tribal self-governance
  • Forest Rights Act 2006: Tribal land and forest rights
  • Transgender Persons Act 2019: Identity recognition and rights

Institutional Mechanisms:

  • National Human Rights Commission: Investigation of rights violations
  • National Commission for SCs/STs: Monitoring implementation of safeguards
  • National Commission for Women: Gender-based rights and protection

Part 4: Development Practice Applications

15 minutes

🔍 Applying Intersectional Analysis to Development Programs

Program Design Questions:

  • Targeting: Who is being reached by our program? Who is being left out?
  • Access: What barriers exist for different identity groups?
  • Participation: Whose voices are centered in program decisions?
  • Outcomes: Are benefits distributed equitably across identity groups?
  • Representation: Who is on our team and in leadership roles?

Intersectional Design Principles:

  • Disaggregate Data: Collect and analyze data by multiple identity categories
  • Nothing About Us Without Us: Ensure meaningful participation of affected communities
  • Address Root Causes: Look beyond symptoms to structural discrimination
  • Build Alliances: Connect across identity groups for collective action
  • Center Most Marginalized: Design from the experiences of those facing multiple discriminations
🏛️ Structural Discrimination

Examples:

  • Caste-based residential segregation
  • Language requirements excluding tribal communities
  • Inaccessible infrastructure for disabled persons
  • Gender-biased family laws

Interventions: Policy advocacy, legal reform, institutional change

👥 Interpersonal Discrimination

Examples:

  • Untouchability practices in schools
  • Religious profiling in employment
  • Disability-based social exclusion
  • Gender-based violence in public spaces

Interventions: Awareness campaigns, sensitivity training, community dialogue

Key Takeaway

Marginalized identities intersect in complex ways that create unique experiences of discrimination and disadvantage. Effective development practice requires understanding these intersections and designing interventions that address multiple, overlapping forms of marginalization simultaneously.
