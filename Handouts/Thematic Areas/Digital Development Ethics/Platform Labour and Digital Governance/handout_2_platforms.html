Platform Governance and Digital Labor in South Asia

Digital Development Ethics 101 - Handout 2

Course: Digital Development Ethics 101
Module: Digital Labor Rights and Platform Economy
Duration: 75-90 minutes
Format: Case study analysis with policy design exercise

Learning Objectives

  • Examine the role of digital platforms in South Asian labor markets
  • Analyze governance challenges in the gig economy
  • Evaluate worker rights in platform-mediated work
  • Design policy frameworks for protecting platform workers

Key Concepts

Platform Economy Dynamics in South Asia:

  • Ride-hailing platforms: Ola, Uber, Pathao, PickMe
  • Food delivery platforms: Swiggy, Zomato, Foodpanda, HungryNaki
  • Microwork platforms: Amazon Mechanical Turk, Clickworker, Microworkers
  • Agricultural platforms: ITC e-Choupal, DeHaat, AgroStar
  • Domestic work platforms: Urban Company, Housejoy, TaskRabbit
  • Freelance platforms: Upwork, Fiverr, Freelancer.com

Case Study: Platform Workers During COVID-19

Context

The COVID-19 pandemic highlighted the vulnerabilities of platform-mediated work across South Asia. While platforms were deemed "essential services," workers faced unprecedented challenges without traditional employment protections.

Key Data Points for Discussion:

  • Employment status: 90% of platform workers in India lack formal employment contracts
  • Income impact: Average earnings decreased 50-70% during lockdowns
  • Health protection: Most workers lacked health insurance or PPE provision
  • Gender impact: Women platform workers faced additional safety and mobility constraints
  • Geographic disparity: Rural delivery workers faced longer distances with same commission rates

Key Issues During the Pandemic:

  • Lack of social protection for gig workers during health crisis
  • Algorithmic management continued despite changed circumstances
  • Platform commission structures remained unchanged despite reduced demand
  • Health and safety responsibilities shifted entirely to workers
  • Inconsistent policy treatment across states and platforms

Discussion Questions

  1. How did platform companies' responses to COVID-19 reveal their understanding of worker relationships?
  2. What role should platforms play in providing social protection during crises?
  3. How did algorithmic management systems adapt (or fail to adapt) to pandemic conditions?
  4. Compare the treatment of platform workers vs. traditional employees during lockdowns.

Activity: Platform Policy Design

Challenge: You are policy advisors tasked with drafting regulations for food delivery platforms in Delhi, Dhaka, or Colombo. Your regulations must balance worker protection with platform innovation and consumer benefits.

Policy Areas to Address:

  • Minimum wage guarantees: How to ensure fair earnings per hour/delivery
  • Insurance requirements: Health, accident, and equipment coverage
  • Transparent algorithm policies: Right to explanation for rating/ranking systems
  • Dispute resolution mechanisms: Fair processes for deactivation appeals
  • Data portability rights: Worker ownership of ratings and performance data

Group Exercise (30 minutes):

Divide into teams representing different stakeholders:

  • Team 1: Platform workers and unions
  • Team 2: Platform companies
  • Team 3: Government regulators
  • Team 4: Consumer advocates

Each team should draft their ideal policy framework, then negotiate a compromise solution.

Comparative Analysis Exercise

Platform Governance Approaches Across South Asia:

India: Social Security Code 2020

  • Recognizes "gig workers" and "platform workers" as separate categories
  • Mandates social security schemes but leaves implementation to states
  • Limited enforcement mechanisms

Pakistan: Limited Regulatory Framework

  • No specific gig work regulations
  • Traditional labor laws don't cover platform work
  • Growing recognition of need for new frameworks

Bangladesh: Emerging Freelancer Protection

  • Focus on international freelance work
  • Payment gateway regulations
  • Limited domestic platform regulation

Sri Lanka: Tech Sector Focus

  • Emphasis on tech industry development
  • Limited attention to gig work protections
  • Traditional employment law framework

Real-World Examples for Analysis

Worker Organizing Efforts:

  • Indian Federation of App-based Transport Workers (IFAT): Cross-platform organizing
  • Gig Workers Collective (India): Social security advocacy
  • All Pakistan Online Taxi Drivers Association: Fare and commission campaigns

Platform Responses:

  • Ola/Uber driver insurance: Limited accident coverage programs
  • Swiggy/Zomato safety initiatives: Training and equipment programs
  • Urban Company background checks: Enhanced verification for domestic workers

Government Initiatives:

  • Delhi government e-auto scheme: Official platform for auto-rickshaw drivers
  • Karnataka platform worker welfare board: State-level protection framework
  • Punjab platform worker registration: Official recognition system

Key Discussion Questions

  1. Labor Rights Translation: How do traditional labor rights (collective bargaining, workplace safety, fair wages) translate to algorithmic management contexts?
  2. Social Protection Role: What role should platforms vs. government vs. workers themselves play in providing social protection?
  3. Worker Organizing: How can worker organizing and collective action occur in platform-mediated contexts where workers rarely meet?
  4. Innovation vs. Protection: How can regulations protect workers without stifling platform innovation or increasing consumer costs?
  5. Informal Economy Integration: How do platform work regulations fit with South Asia's large informal economy?

Research Assignment Options

  1. Platform Worker Survey: Interview 10 platform workers about their experiences, challenges, and policy priorities.
  2. Comparative Policy Analysis: Compare platform governance in one South Asian country with approaches in the EU, California, or Latin America.
  3. Algorithmic Transparency Audit: Analyze how transparent different platforms are about their rating, matching, and payment algorithms.
  4. Gender and Platform Work: Research how platform work affects women differently and what policy interventions could improve gender equity.
