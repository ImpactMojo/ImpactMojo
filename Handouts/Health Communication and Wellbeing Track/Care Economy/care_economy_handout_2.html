Problem Set 1: Care Infrastructure Analysis

Intermediate
🏘️ Urban Planning Case Study: New Residential Development

Context: You're advising on a 5,000-unit affordable housing project in Gurgaon. The developer wants to minimize costs, but the local government wants to ensure the development reduces women's time poverty.

Baseline Data:

  • Target population: 25,000 residents (60% families with children)
  • Current conditions: Women walk 45 minutes daily for water, 30 minutes to nearest school
  • Care burden: Average woman spends 5.2 hours daily on unpaid care work
  • Employment: Only 23% of women in paid employment

Problem 1.1: Infrastructure Priority Analysis

Given limited budget, rank these infrastructure investments by time-saving potential:

💧 Piped Water Supply

Cost: ₹8 crores

Time saving: 45 min/day/woman

Benefits: 12,500 women

Additional: Reduces waterborne diseases

🏫 Community Childcare Center

Cost: ₹5 crores (setup + 5 years operating)

Time saving: 3 hours/day/mother

Benefits: 600 mothers with young children

Additional: Enables women's employment

🛒 Integrated Market Complex

Cost: ₹3 crores

Time saving: 60 min/day/household

Benefits: 5,000 households

Additional: Local employment creation

💰 Cost-Effectiveness Analysis

Calculate cost per hour of time saved annually:

Infrastructure Total Time Saved/Year Cost per Hour Saved Your Ranking
Piped Water 12,500 women × 45 min × 365 days = _____ hours ₹8 crores ÷ _____ hours = ₹_____/hour _____
Childcare Center 600 mothers × 180 min × _____ days = _____ hours ₹5 crores ÷ _____ hours = ₹_____/hour _____
Market Complex 5,000 households × _____ min × 365 days = _____ hours ₹3 crores ÷ _____ hours = ₹_____/hour _____

Problem 1.2: Multiplier Effects Analysis

Beyond direct time savings, consider economic multiplier effects:

🔄 Economic Impact Calculations

Childcare Center Economic Analysis:

  1. Direct time saving: 600 mothers × 3 hours/day × 250 working days = _____ hours/year
  2. Employment enabling: If 40% of mothers enter workforce at ₹15,000/month:
    New employment = 600 × 0.4 = _____ women
    Annual income generated = _____ × ₹15,000 × 12 = ₹_____ crores
  3. Tax revenue: Assuming 10% effective tax rate:
    Additional tax revenue = ₹_____ crores × 0.1 = ₹_____ crores/year
  4. Cost recovery period: ₹5 crores ÷ ₹_____ crores tax/year = _____ years

Policy Insight: The childcare center pays for itself through increased tax revenue in _____ years, while providing ongoing benefits to women and children.

Problem Set 2: Technology and Care Work

Advanced
📱 Digital Solutions for Care Economy

Context: You're designing a comprehensive digital platform to reduce care work burdens for urban working mothers in Bangalore. The platform needs to be financially sustainable and scalable.

🍕 On-Demand Services

Services: Meal delivery, grocery delivery, house cleaning

Time saving: 2 hours/day

Cost to user: ₹300/day

Market size: 50,000 households

👶 Care Coordination

Services: Childcare matching, eldercare services, emergency care

Time saving: 1.5 hours/day + peace of mind

Cost to user: ₹500/month subscription

Market size: 25,000 families

🏠 Smart Home Integration

Services: IoT appliances, automated shopping, energy management

Time saving: 45 minutes/day

Cost to user: ₹10,000 upfront + ₹200/month

Market size: 15,000 tech-savvy households

🎯 Platform Business Model Design (20 minutes)

Step 1: Revenue Model Analysis (8 minutes)

Calculate potential annual revenue for each service:

Service Market Penetration Annual Revenue per User Total Annual Revenue
On-Demand (assume 20% penetration) 50,000 × 0.2 = _____ users ₹300 × 365 = ₹_____ ₹_____ crores
Care Coordination (assume 30% penetration) 25,000 × 0.3 = _____ users ₹500 × 12 = ₹_____ ₹_____ crores
Smart Home (assume 10% penetration) 15,000 × 0.1 = _____ users ₹2,400 × 1 = ₹_____ ₹_____ crores
Step 2: Social Impact Measurement (7 minutes)

Calculate time savings and economic value:

  1. Total time saved annually:
    On-demand: _____ users × 2 hours × 250 days = _____ hours
    Care coordination: _____ users × 1.5 hours × _____ days = _____ hours
    Smart home: _____ users × 0.75 hours × _____ days = _____ hours
    Total: _____ hours saved annually
  2. Economic value of time saved: (Use ₹200/hour opportunity cost)
    Total value = _____ hours × ₹200 = ₹_____ crores
  3. Return on investment: Economic value ÷ Total user spending = ____%
Step 3: Policy Integration (5 minutes)

Design government partnership model:

  1. What subsidies would make services accessible to lower-income families?
  2. How could platform data inform urban planning and care infrastructure?
  3. What regulations are needed to ensure service quality and worker rights?
  4. How could success be measured for policy evaluation?

Advanced
🏛️ State Budget Review: Kerala's Care Economy Investment

  1. Calculate cost per hour saved for each intervention (assume 250 active days/year for 5 years)
  2. Which intervention provides best value for money? _____
  3. Which has highest total impact? _____ (total hours saved)
  4. Recommend your optimal budget allocation:
    • Anganwadi: ₹_____ crores

💡 Integrated Impact Analysis

Beyond time savings, consider:

  • Employment effects: How many women could enter workforce with each intervention?
  • Health impacts: How do water and childcare investments affect family health?
  • Intergenerational effects: How does quality childcare affect children's development?
  • Regional equity: Do allocations address rural-urban disparities?
  • Sustainability: Which investments create lasting infrastructure vs. ongoing costs?

International Best Practices

10 minutes
🇫🇮 Finland: Universal Childcare System

Policy: Universal right to childcare from age 9 months, heavily subsidized by government

Investment: 1.4% of GDP annually

Results: 85% women's employment rate, gender pay gap reduced to 16%, improved child development outcomes

Indian Context: Would require ₹4 lakh crores annually (current total education budget: ₹1.04 lakh crores)

🇰🇷 South Korea: Care Infrastructure Investment

Policy: Massive infrastructure program including community care centers, after-school programs, elder care facilities

Investment: $30 billion over 5 years (2008-2013)

Results: Women's employment increased from 50% to 57%, birth rate stabilized

Indian Context: Proportional investment would be ₹15 lakh crores over 5 years

🇲🇽 Mexico: Estancias Infantiles Program

Policy: Childcare support for working mothers, especially in informal sector

Investment: $200 million annually

Results: 18% increase in women's labor force participation, 13% increase in their income

Indian Context: Scalable model for informal sector workers (92% of women workers)

🤔 Policy Design Questions
  1. Which international model is most applicable to India's federal structure and development priorities?
  2. How could India adapt these models to address its specific challenges (rural-urban divide, caste dynamics, informal economy)?
  3. What role should technology play in scaling care infrastructure efficiently?
  4. How can policies ensure care workers (mostly women) receive decent wages and working conditions?
  5. What indicators would you use to measure the success of care economy investments?

🎯 Workshop Synthesis

Care economy investments are economic development investments. Infrastructure, technology, and services that reduce unpaid care burdens enable women's economic participation, improve family wellbeing, and generate positive returns to government investment. The key is designing integrated approaches that recognize care work as the foundation of all economic activity.
