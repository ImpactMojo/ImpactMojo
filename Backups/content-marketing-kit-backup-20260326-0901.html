ImpactMojo's voice — 5 principles
Specific over vague
"500+ development sector terms, no login required" beats "we have lots of resources." Name the number, the course, the case study.
Practitioner-first
Start from what the practitioner is experiencing — the training gap, the jargon problem, the indicator confusion. ImpactMojo is the answer; the problem comes first.
South Asia context
Reference MGNREGA, ASER, RTI, PARI, NITI Aayog. This signals the platform was built for this audience, not adapted from something else.
No filler confidence
Don't soften claims with "we believe" or "we hope." State what the platform does and let that stand. "48 free courses" needs no qualifier.
Free is the differentiator
Lead with free more than you're comfortable with. In a sector where training costs tens of thousands of rupees, free is a strategic position, not just a price point.
Vandana's personal touch
One line from her own experience at the start or end makes institutional content feel human rather than broadcast.
Phrases to avoid in all captions
✗ "Excited to share..."✗ "We believe learning should be accessible"✗ "Thrilled / delighted / honoured"✗ "Game-changing / transformative"✗ "In today's fast-paced world..."✗ "Don't miss out!"✗ "Empowering practitioners"✗ "Check out our amazing platform!"

"In 15 years of working on capacity building in India's development sector, I've watched the same gap repeat itself. Program managers who are doing sophisticated work — designing surveys, managing evaluations, engaging with government policy — who have never had access to the training that would let them do it more rigorously.

Not because they didn't want to learn. Because the training that exists is either behind expensive institutions or designed for someone else's context entirely.

That's what we built ImpactMojo to fix."
