Transparency & Impact Data

ImpactMojo is built in the open. This page shares our platform metrics, content reach, and feature adoption data. All numbers shown here are static legacy data reconstructed from our codebase audit and historical Google Sheets records.

🔬 Methodology

ImpactMojo tracks platform metrics through two complementary data sources, reconciled into a single view:

Legacy Data (Hardcoded)

Content inventory counts (courses, labs, games, handouts, tools) are derived from a manual audit of the codebase. User counts for courses and games come from historical Google Sheets records maintained since launch. These figures represent cumulative totals and are updated periodically.

Historical Google Sheets

User interaction counts per course, game, and tool are sourced from Google Sheets maintained by the ImpactMojo team since the platform's inception. These spreadsheets track form submissions, enrolment records, and usage logs across all content types.

Reconciliation: The data shown on this page merges both sources. Content counts come from the codebase audit, while user interaction numbers come from historical Google Sheets. All figures are cumulative totals up to the last manual update.

Limitations: Legacy user numbers are approximate (based on Google Forms submissions and manual tracking). No personally identifiable information (PII) is collected or displayed on this page. This page does not include any live GA4 analytics data.

📖 Key Metrics Definitions

These are the standard Google Analytics 4 metrics used by ImpactMojo for internal tracking. This public page shows only legacy/static data, but these definitions explain the metrics we track:

Total Users

The number of distinct users who visited the site during the reporting period. A user is identified by a unique browser cookie or device identifier.

New Users

Users who interacted with the site for the first time during the reporting period. Measured by the first_visit event in GA4.

Sessions

A session begins when a user opens the site and ends after 30 minutes of inactivity or at midnight. A single user can generate multiple sessions.

Pageviews

The total number of pages viewed. Repeated views of the same page are counted. This is the GA4 metric screenPageViews.

Avg Session Duration

The average length of a session in seconds. Calculated as total session duration divided by the number of sessions. Indicates how long users typically spend on the site per visit.

Bounce Rate

The percentage of sessions that were not engaged. A session is considered "bounced" if it lasted less than 10 seconds, had zero key_events, and had fewer than 2 pageviews. Bounce Rate = 1 − Engagement Rate.

Engaged Sessions

The count of sessions that met any of these criteria: lasted 10 seconds or longer, included 1 or more key events (e.g. form submissions, outbound clicks), or included 2 or more pageviews. This is GA4's primary quality-of-visit signal.

Engagement Rate

The percentage of sessions that were engaged. Engagement Rate = Engaged Sessions ÷ Total Sessions. A higher engagement rate indicates users are finding value in the content. This is the inverse of Bounce Rate.

What counts as "Engaged"?

In GA4, a session is engaged if the user stayed for at least 10 seconds, triggered a key event, or viewed 2+ pages. This replaces the older Universal Analytics "bounce" model and provides a more meaningful measure of user interest. For ImpactMojo, engaged sessions typically mean a user explored a course, played a game, or used a tool rather than landing and immediately leaving.

🔒 Legal & Compliance

User counts represent cumulative totals from anonymised form submissions and manual tracking. The GA4 property (used internally) employs anonymised IP collection and complies with India's Information Technology Act, 2000.
