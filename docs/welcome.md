# Welcome to ImpactMojo

**ImpactMojo** is a free learning platform built for people working in development across South Asia — the educators, evaluators, programme managers, researchers, and students who are doing the hard, important work of making communities healthier, more equitable, and more resilient.

**Live site:** [impactmojo.in](https://www.impactmojo.in)

---

## Why ImpactMojo Exists

If you work at an NGO, a think tank, or a university department focused on development, you already know the problem: high-quality training in monitoring & evaluation, research methods, gender analysis, or development economics is either expensive, inaccessible, or designed for audiences in the Global North.

ImpactMojo was built to close that gap. Everything here is:

- **Rooted in South Asian realities** — examples from India, Bangladesh, Nepal, and Sri Lanka, not textbook cases from other contexts
- **Free at the core** — all 48 courses, 12 simulation games, 10 interactive labs, and hundreds of handouts are available without paying a rupee
- **Designed for practitioners** — not abstract theory, but content you can use in your next workshop, field visit, or strategy meeting
- **Available offline** — because we know internet connections in the field aren't always reliable
- **Multilingual** — available in English, Hindi, Tamil, Bengali, Telugu, and Marathi

No login is required to start learning. Just open the website and begin.

## What You'll Find Here

ImpactMojo is much more than a course library. Here's what's available:

### Courses
**9 flagship courses** (each with ~13 in-depth modules) covering topics like MEL for Development, Theory of Change, and Development Economics. Plus **39 foundational courses** — shorter, focused lessons on specific skills and concepts. All organized across 6 learning tracks.

### Economics Simulation Games
**12 interactive games** that let you experience economic concepts by making decisions — budget allocations, market dynamics, policy trade-offs. Ideal for workshops and classrooms.

### Interactive Labs
**10 browser-based workbenches** where you can build frameworks, analyse data, and produce real outputs (like a theory of change diagram or a research question) step by step.

### Handouts
**400+ downloadable pages** organized by track — ready to print, share in a training, or assign as pre-reading.

### ImpactLex
A **dictionary of 500+ development terms** — from "attribution" to "zero-based budgeting" — available as an app that works offline.

### Dev Case Studies
**200 curated case studies** drawn from real development programmes in **117 countries**. Search by theme, country, or methodology.

### DevDiscourses
**500+ curated academic papers, books, and reports** — the essential reading list for development professionals, organized so you can actually find what you need.

### Dataverse
**215+ datasets, APIs, tools, and MCP servers** for development research. If you need data on poverty, health outcomes, governance indicators, or climate — start here.

### Blog & Podcast
Regular writing and audio on development practice, new platform features, and sector trends.

### Certificates
Complete courses and earn certificates to demonstrate your learning — useful for professional development records and team reporting.

## How to Get Started

**You don't need to install anything.** ImpactMojo runs entirely in your web browser.

1. **Visit [impactmojo.in](https://www.impactmojo.in)** on your phone, tablet, or computer
2. **Browse the course catalog** or pick a learning track that matches your work
3. **Start learning** — no account required for free content
4. **Create a free account** (optional) to track your progress, bookmark resources, and earn certificates

For a detailed walkthrough of your first visit, see the [Getting Started Guide](getting-started.md).

## For Developers and Contributors

If you're here to contribute to the codebase, translations, or content, welcome! ImpactMojo is an open-source project.

### Quick Start

```bash
git clone https://github.com/Varnasr/ImpactMojo.git
cd ImpactMojo
python -m http.server 8000
# Open http://localhost:8000
```

The site is static HTML/CSS/JS with no build step — just clone and serve. See the [Architecture](architecture.md) and [Contributing](contributing.md) guides for details.
