# Why ImpactMojo

A guide for anyone evaluating ImpactMojo — whether you're an NGO training lead deciding where to send your team, a university lecturer looking for course materials, or a funder assessing capacity-building partners.

---

## The Problem

Development professionals in South Asia face a training gap:

- **University curricula lag practice** — graduates learn theory but not the MEL frameworks, data tools, and policy analysis skills employers need.
- **Quality training is expensive** — a 3-day MEAL workshop from international providers costs $500–$2,000 per person. Most grassroots NGOs cannot afford this.
- **Content is Western-centric** — the dominant platforms (Coursera, edX) teach development from a Global North perspective with Global North case studies.
- **No single platform covers the full stack** — organizations patch together content from a dozen sources, losing coherence and creating gaps.

## What ImpactMojo Does Differently

### 1. Genuinely free, not freemium

48 courses, 12 games, 10 labs, 400+ handouts, 215+ data tools, 500+ curated papers — all free. No login wall, no trial period, no feature gating. The free tier is the real product.

Premium tools exist for advanced practitioners (qualitative analysis, AI transcription, code conversion) and sustain the platform, but 95% of the content is free forever.

### 2. Built for South Asia, relevant everywhere

Every course uses case studies from India, Bangladesh, Nepal, Sri Lanka, and the broader South Asian context. Topics like the Indian Constitution, NREGA, caste and marginalization, and South Asian climate resilience don't exist on other platforms.

Content is available in 6 languages: English, Hindi, Tamil, Bengali, Telugu, and Marathi.

### 3. Full-stack development education

ImpactMojo is the only platform that covers all six pillars of development practice in one place:

| Track | What practitioners learn |
|-------|------------------------|
| MEL & Research | Theory of change, indicators, data collection, impact evaluation, adaptive management |
| Data & Technology | Data literacy, EDA, econometrics, visualization, AI for M&E |
| Policy & Economics | Development economics, political economy, cost-effectiveness, fundraising |
| Gender & Equity | Gender mainstreaming, feminist research, data feminism, intersectionality |
| Health & Communication | Public health, maternal health, BCC, climate resilience, SEL |
| Philosophy & Governance | Constitutional law, digital ethics, decolonization, environmental justice |

### 4. Learn by doing, not just reading

- **12 economics games** teach behavioral economics, game theory, and market failures through interactive simulations
- **10 labs** let practitioners build theories of change, design MEL systems, and practice policy advocacy hands-on
- **35+ Dojos** are 90-minute practice sessions where skills are drilled, not lectured

### 5. Workshop-ready

Every resource is designed to be used in group training, not just self-study:

- Handouts work as pre-reads and takeaways
- Games can be projected in workshops for group play
- Labs produce exportable outputs (PDFs, PNGs) for reports and proposals
- Facilitator guides are available for popular workshop topics

### 6. Rigorous but accessible

Content is designed by Dr. Varna Sri Raman, a development economist with field experience across South Asia. Courses are academically rigorous (interactive lexicons, curated reading lists from 500+ papers) but written for practitioners, not academics.

## Competitive Advantages

### vs. Coursera / edX / FutureLearn
- ImpactMojo is **100% free** for core content (no audit-mode limitations)
- **South Asian case studies** vs. Western-centric examples
- **Practitioner-focused** vs. academic lecture format
- **Interactive labs and games** vs. video + quiz format
- **6 Indian languages** vs. English-only

### vs. International training providers (CLEAR, IPDET, J-PAL MicroMasters)
- **No cost barrier** — J-PAL MicroMasters costs $1,350; ImpactMojo covers similar ground for free
- **Broader scope** — ImpactMojo covers policy, gender, governance, and health alongside MEL/data
- **Always available** — no cohort schedules or application processes
- **South Asian focus** — not a global program with token regional examples

### vs. In-house NGO training
- **Consistent quality** — pre-built, tested content vs. ad-hoc slide decks
- **Scales to any team size** — from 1 intern to 500 field staff
- **Saves facilitator prep time** — handouts, games, and labs are ready to use
- **Measurable** — track completion and progress with the Team Dashboard

## For Funders and Partners

ImpactMojo offers a high-leverage investment in capacity building:

- **Cost per learner**: Effectively zero for the free tier. Organization tier at ₹1,499/user/month is a fraction of traditional training costs.
- **Reach**: Content is accessible to anyone with an internet connection and a browser. No app downloads, no special hardware.
