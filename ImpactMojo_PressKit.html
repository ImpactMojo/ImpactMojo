Extended (for features and profiles)

ImpactMojo is a free, open-source development education platform designed to democratize access to rigorous, practitioner-grade knowledge across South Asia and the Global South. Founded by Dr. Varna Sri Raman and Vandana Soni and supported by PinPoint Ventures, the platform hosts 48+ courses spanning development economics, monitoring and evaluation, gender studies, constitutional law, climate, public health, and more. Flagship courses run 12-13 modules with interactive lexicons, NotebookLM AI study companions, and South Asian case studies. Alongside courses, ImpactMojo offers six open-access reference libraries, 16 interactive economics games, 9 live case challenges sourced from partner NGOs, and a suite of premium research tools including AI transcription in 10 or more South Asian languages and realistic development datasets with 840,000 or more rows of practice data. The platform is released under the MIT License, IT Act 2000 compliant, and used by practitioners from the UN, World Bank, bilateral donors, national NGOs, research institutes, and universities across the region.

Platform Context — The Story

Why ImpactMojo exists

Context for journalists writing about the platform's mission and founding.

"After years of field research and teaching across South Asia, we kept encountering the same frustration: rigorous development education was either locked behind expensive degrees or scattered across dense academic papers. Practitioners in the field — the people actually implementing programs — deserved better. We built ImpactMojo to bridge that gap. Rigorous content with practitioner-level accessibility. Every course, lab, and game is designed for people who need to apply these concepts tomorrow, not just understand them in theory." — Dr. Varna Sri Raman, Founder

The gap being addressed
Development education is either expensive (degrees, workshops) or inaccessible (academic journals). Practitioners need applied, cited, affordable knowledge — and until ImpactMojo, no platform was built specifically for them.
Why free at the core
The practitioners who need rigorous knowledge most — NGO staff, government officers, early-career researchers — are also those with the smallest training budgets. Paywalls compound inequality in the sector.
South Asia focus
Most development education platforms use Western case studies. ImpactMojo is built with MGNREGA, ASER, NHM, and India's constitutional law at the centre — not as afterthoughts.
Open source rationale
MIT License ensures no vendor lock-in, enables verification of data practices, and makes the platform auditable. This is a deliberate design choice, not incidental.
