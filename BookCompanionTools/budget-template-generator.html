Budget Best Practices

Personnel Costs

  • Salary benchmarking: Use sector salary surveys (e.g., Indian Development Sector Salary Survey) for realistic figures.
  • Include statutory benefits: PF, gratuity, medical insurance (add 20-30% on top of gross salary in India).
  • Consultants: Budget daily rates; short-term experts are often more cost-effective than full-time hires for specialized tasks.

Funder-Specific Guidance

  • Bilateral (USAID/DFID): Indirect costs typically negotiated at 10-15%. Require detailed cost notes.
  • Multilateral (UN/WB): Often use harmonized budget templates. Programme support costs usually 7-8%.
  • Foundations: More flexible on overhead (8-15%). Focus on cost-effectiveness narrative.
  • Government: Strict line-item budgets. Overhead usually capped at 5-8%.
  • CSR: Prefer low admin costs (under 10%). Strong focus on direct beneficiary spend.

Realistic Costing for South Asian Contexts

  • Factor in seasonal accessibility issues (monsoon travel costs, field activity windows).
  • Community mobilizer stipends: INR 8,000-15,000/month (varies by state and urban/rural).
  • Training workshop costs: INR 500-1,500 per participant per day (including refreshments, venue, materials).
  • Always budget a 3-5% contingency for exchange rate fluctuations and price escalation.
