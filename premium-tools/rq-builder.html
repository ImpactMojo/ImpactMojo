What are you investigating?

Describe your research area in plain language. Don't worry about structure yet.

Be as specific as you can about the population, intervention, and outcome you're interested in.

Choose Your Framework

Select the framework that best fits your research type.

PICO Components

Break your question into its core components.

Who are you studying? Be specific about geography, demographics, and eligibility.

What treatment, programme, or exposure are you examining?

What is the counterfactual? What happens without the intervention?

What measurable result are you looking for?

SPIDER Components

Break your question into its core components.

Who or what is your sample? Describe the group you're studying.

What phenomenon, behaviour, or experience are you investigating?

How will you evaluate or analyse the data?

Refine Your Question

Review and polish your research question. Edit it to be clear, specific, and feasible.

Edit freely. A good RQ is specific, measurable, and answerable with available data.
