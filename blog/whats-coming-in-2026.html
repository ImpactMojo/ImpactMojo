What's Coming in 2026

The ImpactMojo Team December 2, 2025 5 min read
South Asia capacity building

When we launched ImpactMojo, we had a simple goal: make quality development sector education accessible to practitioners across South Asia, regardless of where they work or what they can afford.

As 2025 draws to a close, we're grateful for every learner who has trusted us with their professional development. Now we want to share what's next—not as promises carved in stone, but as a roadmap we're genuinely excited to pursue.

The Year Ahead

Our roadmap for 2026 focuses on three themes: deeper tools for research and evaluation, richer content across more languages and topics, and stronger community through coaching and peer learning.

Q1 2026 Research Ops Suite
📊

Power & Sample Lab Coming

Sample size calculators with cluster design, ICC adjustments, and attrition buffers—designed for real field conditions in South Asia.

📋

Survey Cost Estimator Coming

Budget planning tool with region-specific benchmarks in INR, covering enumerator costs, travel, supervision, and more.

🗓️

Field Timeline Planner Coming

Realistic scheduling with monsoon seasons, festival calendars, and agricultural cycles built in.

Q2 2026 Content Expansion
🎓

Advanced Evaluation Methods

New courses covering difference-in-differences, regression discontinuity, synthetic controls, and when to use each.

🌐

Regional Language Courses

Full course content in Tamil, Telugu, Bengali, and Marathi—not just subtitles, but native instruction.

📚

Case Study Library

Real evaluation examples from South Asian organisations, with permission, showing what worked and what didn't.

Q3–Q4 Community & Support
👥

Peer Learning Cohorts

Small groups working through courses together with structured discussion and accountability.

🎯

Expert Office Hours

Regular sessions with experienced practitioners to discuss real challenges and get practical guidance.

🏆

Skills Certification

Verifiable credentials that demonstrate practical competency, not just course completion.

What's Available Now

While we build toward 2026, here's what you can explore today:

📋

Logframe Lab Live

Build and validate logical frameworks with guided steps and smart suggestions.

🔄

Theory of Change Builder Live

Visual ToC creation with assumption mapping and pathway validation.

📊

Sample Size Calculator Beta

Quick calculations for common evaluation designs with clear explanations.

🎯

Indicator Workbench Live

Design SMART indicators with built-in quality checks and examples.

Built With You

Everything on our roadmap comes from conversations with practitioners. The Research Ops Suite emerged from M&E managers telling us they spend hours on sample size calculations that should take minutes. The regional language expansion reflects feedback that English-only content excludes many capable professionals.

How You Can Shape What's Next

  • Use the labs: Your usage patterns tell us what works and what needs improvement
  • Send feedback: The "Feedback" button isn't decoration—we read everything
  • Join beta testing: Early access to new tools in exchange for honest input
  • Share your challenges: The problems you face become the features we build

Staying Accessible

As we grow, we're committed to keeping our core content free. The courses, the labs, the games—these remain open to anyone who wants to learn.

Premium features like advanced coaching, certification, and organisational tools will help sustain the platform. But the educational mission comes first. A programme officer in rural Odisha and a consultant in Singapore should both be able to build the same foundational skills.

"Quality development education shouldn't be a luxury good available only to those who can attend expensive workshops in metropolitan cities."

The Bigger Picture

Our ultimate goal isn't to build a platform—it's to build capacity across the sector. Every practitioner who strengthens their skills improves the programmes they work on, which benefits the communities those programmes serve.

If ImpactMojo helps even a fraction of the development workforce do their work more effectively, that's a multiplier effect worth pursuing.
